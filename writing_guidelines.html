Integrating AI in Coursework

Following UW Writing Course Guidelines

Situate Writing Instruction in Context
Examples of acceptable AI use for discipline, audience, and genre, and how to critically evaluate outputs.

For your lab report introduction, you may use AI to help generate a draft, but you must revise it to match the conventions and expectations of your discipline and audience. Highlight your changes and explain your reasoning.

Critical Thinking Example: After receiving an AI-generated summary, compare it to your own understanding of the text. Identify any inaccuracies, missing context, or bias in the AI output, and write a reflection on how you corrected or improved the summary to better fit your course's writing standards.

Use AI to generate a summary of a primary source, then compare the AI output to your own summary. Discuss which better fits the genre and communication norms of your field and why.

For a legal memo, use AI to outline the structure, but ensure all arguments and citations are your own. Critically evaluate the AI's outline for accuracy and appropriateness.

When using AI for brainstorming, document which ideas you kept, modified, or rejected, and explain how you determined their fit for the assignment's context.

Articulate Learning Goals for Writing
Examples of how students can use AI prompting to meet learning goals and how to critically evaluate AI outputs.

Use AI to generate a list of possible arguments for your essay topic. Critically evaluate which arguments are most relevant to your assignment goals and explain your choices.

Critical Thinking Example: After AI suggests arguments, check each for logical soundness, evidence, and alignment with your assignment's learning goals. Write a short analysis of why you accepted or rejected each suggestion, and how this process deepened your understanding of the topic.

Ask AI to provide feedback on your thesis statement or topic sentences. Revise your work based on both AI and instructor feedback, and reflect on which suggestions you accepted or rejected and why.

Use AI to create a step-by-step plan for drafting your literature review. As you follow the plan, note where AI suggestions were helpful or needed adjustment to fit your course objectives.

After using AI to outline your essay, compare the AI-generated outline to your instructor’s rubric or learning objectives. Identify any gaps and revise your outline accordingly.

Clarify Independent vs. AI-Assisted Goals
Help students distinguish which writing goals should be met on their own and which can involve AI support.

List the writing goals for this assignment and indicate which ones must be completed independently and which can use AI assistance (e.g., brainstorming vs. final analysis).

Critical Thinking Example: When using AI for a specific writing goal, pause to ask: Does the AI output demonstrate deep understanding, or is it superficial? Write a reflection on how you ensured your independent work met higher-order thinking and learning goals, and how you improved upon or challenged the AI's suggestions.

Provide a table that separates writing tasks (e.g., outlining, drafting, revising) into those that require original student work and those where AI support is allowed.

Explain why thesis development should be done independently, but AI can be used for generating topic ideas or organizing research notes.

Describe how students should document their use of AI for specific writing goals in their process log or reflection.

Identify Writing Goals and AI Support
Identify specific writing goals and explain how AI tools can support practice and feedback.

For a literature review, use AI to practice summarizing sources, but write the synthesis and analysis sections independently.

Critical Thinking Example: After using AI to summarize a source, check for accuracy, nuance, and bias. Write a brief critique of the AI summary, noting what you would add or change to meet your instructor's expectations for critical analysis.

Ask AI to provide feedback on the clarity of your topic sentences, then revise them based on both AI and instructor comments.

Use AI to generate sample outlines for a persuasive essay, but ensure the arguments and evidence are your own.

Practice paraphrasing with AI-generated examples, then write your own paraphrase and compare the two for accuracy and originality.

Build Reflection on AI Use
Include reflection questions that ask students to explain how they used AI to meet writing goals and assess its effectiveness.

Reflect on which writing goals you met with AI support and which you completed independently. What did you learn from each approach?

Critical Thinking Example: After using AI, write a reflection on how the AI's suggestions influenced your thinking. Did it challenge your assumptions, or did you find flaws in its logic? How did you use this insight to improve your own writing and learning?

Describe a specific instance where AI feedback improved your draft. How did you decide what to revise?

Assess the effectiveness of AI-generated suggestions for your assignment. Which were most helpful and why?

Explain how you balanced your own ideas with AI input to achieve the assignment's learning objectives.

Support Writing as a Staged Process
Break tasks into smaller steps and ask for iterative feedback.

First, brainstorm five potential thesis statements on the impact of artificial intelligence in education.

Critical Thinking Example: After AI generates thesis statements, evaluate each for clarity, originality, and alignment with your assignment. Write a short justification for your chosen thesis, explaining why it best meets the criteria and how you improved it beyond the AI's suggestion.

Now that we have a thesis, generate a detailed outline with introduction, three evidence‐based sections, and conclusion.

Review this draft introduction and provide feedback on clarity and organization, highlighting areas to expand or revise.

Use AI at Different Writing Stages
Encourage students to use AI for brainstorming, organizing, and editing at appropriate stages.

Use AI to brainstorm possible research questions or essay topics before starting your draft.

Critical Thinking Example: When AI suggests research questions, analyze which are most relevant and why. Write a brief rationale for your selection, and note how you refined or combined AI ideas to better fit your assignment's goals.

Ask AI to help organize your main points into a logical outline during the planning stage.

Request AI feedback on sentence clarity and grammar during the final editing stage.

Use AI to suggest transitions between sections after your first draft is complete.

Reflect on AI's Role in the Writing Process
Assign short reflections for students to explain how AI contributed to their writing process.

Write a brief reflection on how AI helped you brainstorm or organize your ideas for this assignment.

Critical Thinking Example: After using AI for feedback, identify one suggestion you disagreed with and explain why. How did you use your own judgment to make a better revision? What did this process teach you about your writing?

Describe a specific revision you made after receiving AI feedback and explain your reasoning.

Reflect on the strengths and limitations of AI assistance at different stages of your writing process.

Explain how you balanced your own ideas with AI suggestions throughout drafting and revising.

Clarify Student Responsibility for Content
Make it clear that students are responsible for all content and final revisions, even when using AI.

Remind students that AI can assist with mechanics and structure, but they must ensure all content is accurate and original.

Critical Thinking Example: After revising AI-generated text, highlight areas where you made significant changes. Write a short reflection on why your changes were necessary to meet academic integrity and assignment expectations.

Require students to review and revise all AI-generated text to meet assignment expectations and academic integrity standards.

Ask students to highlight or annotate sections where they made significant changes to AI suggestions.

Include a statement in the assignment instructions that students are ultimately responsible for the quality and integrity of their work.

Assess Writing Equitably
Design prompts that reduce bias and encourage inclusive language.

Suggest revisions to this paragraph to ensure a neutral tone and avoid cultural or gender bias.

Critical Thinking Example: When AI suggests language or examples, check for bias or exclusion. Write a short analysis of how you revised the AI output to be more inclusive and equitable, and how this supports the course's writing guidelines.

Provide three alternative wordings that maintain academic rigor but are accessible for multilingual students.

Generate a peer‐review checklist for an argumentative essay that emphasizes evidence, organization, and respectful tone.

Alternative Assessments & Self-Evaluations
Incorporate self-evaluations and alternative assessments for students to explain their writing decisions and AI use.

Submit a self-evaluation describing how you used (or chose not to use) AI tools at each stage of your writing process and why.

Critical Thinking Example: In your self-evaluation, discuss a moment where you disagreed with AI feedback. How did you resolve the difference, and what did you learn about your own writing process and the assignment's goals?

With your final draft, include a reflection explaining which writing decisions were influenced by AI feedback, how you revised based on it, and what you learned about your own writing.

In a portfolio, annotate your drafts to show where you used AI, what you changed, and your reasoning for accepting or rejecting AI suggestions.

Complete a rubric section justifying your use of AI and reflecting on how it contributed to meeting assignment goals, or why you chose not to use it.

Support Diverse Learners & AI Access
Provide equitable access and guided practice for students with different levels of AI experience.

Participate in a class workshop where you practice using AI for brainstorming or revision, then reflect on what you found helpful or challenging.

Critical Thinking Example: After a workshop, compare your approach to AI with a peer's. What did you do differently, and why? How did your choices reflect your understanding of the writing guidelines and your learning needs?

Review provided tutorials on AI-assisted writing and discuss in a short response how you might use or avoid AI in your coursework.

For this assignment, you may choose to use or not use AI. In your submission, include a brief statement explaining your choice and its impact on your process.

Complete a survey at the start of the course about your experience and comfort with AI tools; revisit your responses at the end of the course and reflect on your growth.

Evaluate Writing Based on Goals & Learning
Assess writing on how well it communicates ideas, meets assignment goals, and reflects student learning, regardless of AI use.

Your work will be graded using a rubric that prioritizes clarity, organization, and achievement of learning objectives. AI use is permitted only if it is documented and critically evaluated in your reflection.

Critical Thinking Example: When submitting your work, include a paragraph explaining how you evaluated the strengths and weaknesses of both your own and AI-generated writing. How did you ensure your final draft best met the assignment's learning goals?

Provide a brief statement with your submission explaining how your final draft demonstrates your learning and growth, including your use of AI if applicable.

Participate in peer review focused on communication and goal achievement. Include prompts for discussing how AI was or was not used in the writing process.

Critically evaluate an example of AI-generated writing and a student-generated writing. Discuss which better meets the assignment goals and why.

Demonstrate Professional AI Use
Show how professionals in your field might use AI tools for writing-related tasks.

As a historian, use AI to generate a summary of primary source documents for a research article.

Critical Thinking Example: After using AI for a professional writing task, compare the AI output to published examples in your field. What did the AI miss or get wrong? How did you revise the text to meet professional standards and learning outcomes?

As an engineer, use AI to draft a technical project update email for a team meeting.

As a business analyst, use AI to outline a market research report based on recent data.

As a sociologist, use AI to help organize interview transcripts into thematic categories for qualitative analysis.

Clarify Appropriate AI Use
Clarify when and how AI is appropriate for writing in your discipline.

List scenarios where AI-generated text is acceptable in scientific writing and where it is not.

Critical Thinking Example: For each scenario, explain your reasoning for when AI use is appropriate or not. How does your decision align with ethical, disciplinary, and learning guidelines?

Explain the ethical considerations of using AI for drafting legal memos in law school.

Describe when it is appropriate to use AI for brainstorming ideas in a creative writing course.

Provide department guidelines for citing or disclosing AI assistance in academic writing.

Share Example Texts With and Without AI
Show students example texts created with and without AI assistance to clarify expectations.

Compare an AI-generated lab report introduction to a student-written introduction and discuss strengths and weaknesses.

Critical Thinking Example: After reviewing both AI and student examples, write a reflection on which better demonstrates critical thinking, clarity, and alignment with the assignment's goals. What would you change in each to improve learning outcomes?

Show a side-by-side comparison of a literature review section written by a student and one generated by AI.

Provide an example of a business email drafted by AI and one revised by a professional, highlighting key differences.

Analyze a policy brief written with AI support and one written independently, focusing on tone and accuracy.

Design Assignments for Course Level & Context
Create assignments that match the course level and emphasize writing effort and disciplinary context.

Design a first-year assignment that asks students to use AI for brainstorming but requires original drafting and revision.

Critical Thinking Example: After completing the assignment, reflect on how using AI for brainstorming influenced your writing process. Did it help or hinder your learning? How did you ensure your final product met the course's writing guidelines?

Create an upper-level assignment where students compare their own writing process with and without AI assistance.

Develop a group project where students must document how AI was used at each stage of a technical report.

Assign a reflective essay on the challenges and benefits of using AI in discipline-specific writing tasks.
