AI Prompting Tips

Based on UW Writing Course Guidelines

Click on each guideline card to reveal AI‐prompting tips that follow best practices in writing instruction.

Situate Writing Instruction in Context
Specify discipline, audience, and genre when crafting AI prompts.

As a sociology professor, draft a research question proposal that explains how urbanization affects social networks.

Write a lab‐report introduction for a chemistry experiment on titration, targeting first‐year undergraduates.

Generate a legal argument outline for a memorandum on privacy law compliance, assuming the audience is a law‐firm partner.

Articulate Learning Goals for Writing
Embed clear objectives and outcomes in your AI prompts.

Help me critically evaluate arguments about climate change; identify main claims, evidence, and potential counterarguments.

Create a step‐by‐step guide on how to draft a literature review, emphasizing thesis development and source integration.

Outline the learning objectives and assessment criteria for a persuasive essay on renewable energy, suitable for a sophomore writing course.

Support Writing as a Staged Process
Break tasks into smaller steps and ask for iterative feedback.

First, brainstorm five potential thesis statements on the impact of artificial intelligence in education.

Now that we have a thesis, generate a detailed outline with introduction, three evidence‐based sections, and conclusion.

Review this draft introduction and provide feedback on clarity and organization, highlighting areas to expand or revise.

Assess Writing Equitably
Design prompts that reduce bias and encourage inclusive language.

Suggest revisions to this paragraph to ensure a neutral tone and avoid cultural or gender bias.

Provide three alternative wordings that maintain academic rigor but are accessible for multilingual students.

Generate a peer‐review checklist for an argumentative essay that emphasizes evidence, organization, and respectful tone.
