What is Prompting?

Prompting is the process of entering information, questions, or instructions (“prompts”) into a Generative AI tool to guide its responses. The quality and specificity of your prompts have a major impact on the usefulness and accuracy of the AI’s output. After you submit a prompt, the AI analyzes your input and generates a response based on patterns it has learned during training.

How to Create Effective Prompts

Be specific: Clearly describe what you want. Instead of “Write a story,” try “Write a short, funny story for children about a robot who learns to dance.”

Provide context: Add details about the topic, audience, tone, or format you want.

Ask the AI to take on a role: Such as “Act as if you are a personal trainer.”

Specify the format: Tell AI how to present the response, like “Provide a bullet-point summary.”

List what you want included or excluded: Add instructions such as “Do include tomatoes. Don’t include wheat.”

Give examples: Share a sample sentence or paragraph to show the style or structure you want (but avoid copyrighted material).

Consider tone and audience: Specify who the output is for and the tone you want, like “Make it formal and suitable for business communication.”

Iterate and refine: Start with a basic prompt and build on it, adding more detail or correcting mistakes as you go.

Ask for help: If you’re unsure, ask the AI to suggest prompts or tell you what information it needs.

Why Prompting Matters

Well-crafted prompts help you get more accurate, relevant, and useful results from AI tools. They reduce misunderstandings and improve the quality of the AI’s responses. Remember, AI-generated content can sometimes be inaccurate, misleading, or inappropriate, so always review and verify outputs before using or sharing them.

Note: Before using AI tools for work or study, review your organization’s guidelines. For example, Harvard provides specific guidance and collections of prompts for educational use.
