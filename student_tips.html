AI Prompting Tips for Students

Click on a guideline card to reveal student‑focused AI‑prompting tips that follow UW W‑course best practices.

Link to Your Course Context
Show how your assignment’s subject and audience shape your prompt.

I am a biology student writing about photosynthesis. Generate three concise research questions suitable for my lab report.

Compose a memo for my English 200 class explaining the main themes of 'To Kill a Mockingbird' aimed at classmates unfamiliar with the novel.

As a history major, summarize key causes of the Civil War in bullet points for a study guide.

Define Your Writing Objectives
Specify what you want to learn or demonstrate with your prompt.

Help me identify evidence for a persuasive essay on renewable energy, focusing on credible sources and statistics.

Outline the steps to draft a literature review for sociology, including thesis development and source integration.

Provide an academic writing checklist so I can self‑assess structure, argument strength, and use of citations.

Break Down Tasks into Steps
Ask for iterative help—brainstorm, outline, draft, revise.

First, list five possible thesis statements for my essay on digital literacy in education.

Now that I have a thesis, create a detailed outline with introduction, three evidence sections, and conclusion.

Review this draft body paragraph and give suggestions to improve clarity and transitions.

Ensure Inclusive, Fair Language
Craft prompts that avoid bias and honor diverse perspectives.

Suggest word choices to make this paragraph clear for non‑native English speakers without losing academic tone.

Revise this introduction to remove culturally biased language and maintain a respectful, neutral tone.

Provide feedback on this argument to ensure it’s logically organized and avoids assumptions about different backgrounds.
