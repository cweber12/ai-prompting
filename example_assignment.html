Children’s Literature Book Review

Course: BEDUC 250 – Topics in Education and Popular Culture
Instructor: Dr. Antony Smith (smithant@uw.edu)
Quarter: Spring 2025, University of Washington Bothell

For this assignment, you will choose one of the four children’s books you read and write a review of it in three parts:

  1. Book summary
  2. Personal response
  3. Critical connections

To write your review you will use both large language model Artificial Intelligence and your own words and ideas. Here are the steps you will need to follow to complete the assignment:

  1. Prompt ChatGPT or a similar text generation AI tool to write a draft review

    This step aligns with Guideline 2: Articulate Learning Goals for Writing and the recommendation to explore various technologies for composing, helping students use digital tools to support their writing process.

  2. Copy and paste this review into a blank document
  3. Read the review and revise/edit it in three ways, using track changes:
    1. Style: Delete, add, or revise for strange words or awkward phrasing

      This sub-step aligns with Guideline 3: Support Writing as a Staged Process by emphasizing revision and iterative feedback, which are essential for developing strong writing skills.

    2. Voice: Would you use those words or phrases? If not, change words and phrases to sound like something you would actually say

      This sub-step aligns with Guideline 3: Support Writing as a Staged Process by honoring each student’s personal voice and encouraging authentic expression in academic writing.

    3. Content: Is the review accurate? Does it leave out something important or get some details wrong? Add or revise phrases or sentences as needed to make the review clear and complete

      This sub-step aligns with Guideline 3: Support Writing as a Staged Process by using revision to develop clarity, accuracy, and critical thinking in student writing.

  4. Add a paragraph exploring your own personal response to the story

    This step aligns with Guideline 3: Support Writing as a Staged Process by honoring students’ experiences, languages, and reflections as valuable contributions to academic work.

  5. Add a paragraph discussing this title as a banned book: Why do you think it was banned? What might the impact be on libraries, schools, or children if this book is no longer on the shelves? Which of our course themes seem most relevant to this book?

    This step aligns with Guideline 2: Articulate Learning Goals for Writing by encouraging students to make critical connections, engage in higher-order thinking, and reflect on how their writing addresses course learning objectives and disciplinary standards.

  6. Turn in this three-paragraph book review (showing the changes tracked) to Canvas.
  7. You are also encouraged to post your book review (with changes accepted) to Amazon or Goodreads, either as yourself or anonymously. If you do this, please also post the link to your online review to Canvas.

All three parts together should be 250+ words.
